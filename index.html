Favorite Sounds

These are the tones, details, lines, and moments that caught my ear this year. There are sounds excerpted from 58 songs, loosely grouped by theme into three collections, a total of 12 minutes.

Part 1: Vocal-musical Connections

  • “Neighbors’ music through the concrete…” and the muted orchestral sample to match. Prophecy by Magdalena Bay
  • Line punctuated by synth flute. The Return by Lightning Bug
  • Rising strings. Hyacinth (Strings) by serpentwithfeet
  • Quick little guitar flourish. Montero (Call Me By Your Name) by Lil Nas X
  • Drum roll/guitar roil. Give Me Fifteen by Aimee Mann
  • The echo on “huh?” and that pissed-off synth line. Deja Vu by Olivia Rodrigo
  • Slowed-down, echoed vocal loop. Did We Say Goodbye by Mr. Mitch
  • Time-stretched outro. Back3School by Porches
  • Pitched vocal samples in the calm after the storm. Days Like These by Low
  • Syncopated descending vocal sample. Halfway by Magdalena Bay
  • Stretched-out “Toniiiiiiiigght!” sample. Pick ‘em Up by Eris Drew
  • Echo on the vocals: “do you think of me (me)” Two Face by L’Rain
  • Killer Mike’s “Ha-ha-ha-ha-ha!” punctuated with kicks Ooh La La (Mexican Institute of Sound Remix) by Run the Jewels feat. Mexican Institute of Sound & Santa Fe Klan
  • Vocal vaporized. The Daisy by Ross from Friends
  • Vocal scrambled. Look at the Sky by Porter Robinson
  • Singing along with the guitar part. Where the Lightning Strikes Twice by Foxing
  • Brief jump into double-time kick-snare pattern. Y DON'T U by Park Hye Jin
  • Filtered, effected “You’ll get your chance to move your feet.” Bussifame by Dawn
  • “Let me, let me” layered vocal loop. Clearing by Dolphin Midwives

Part 2: Neat Sounds

  • Pop! …and rebuild. Poison Pudding by Parris feat. Call Super
  • Pitch-bent hi-hats. Never the Right Time by Andy Stott feat. Allison Skidmore
  • “Uh-luh” percussive vocal sample. Daybreak by Bapari
  • That resonant pluck bouncing around the stereo field. 5ives by Sully
  • Sudden drums, then again, reversed. Every Weather by Leif
  • Ping-ponging snare roll+rimshot. Hidden by Horizons by Lone feat. Morgane Diet
  • Claps pitched and echoing into space. Oulpa Tvi by Robag Wruhme
  • Vocoded, reverbed, clicky little vocal snippet. Soft Spot by Piri
  • Sudden Amen break after four minutes of ambient drift. Death Engine (The Field Remix) by I Break Horses
  • Sudden Amen break after two minutes of build-up. Stairzzzzzz by Machinedrum
  • Piano loop from the fog (perhaps a sample of Ludovico Einaudi?) Einaudi by Nadia Struiwigh
  • Fluttering, clopping percussion. Drin by Christina Chatfield
  • Wood-block-y sound on the upbeat and the synth trill spilling out. Quartz Register by Emissive
  • That writhing, slick synth lead. Unravel in the Designated Zone by Anz
  • Plinks and plonks riding the delay pedal. Prophet Idea/End Credits by Shek O
  • Filtered echo on the strings. Gloria by Angel Olsen
  • Steel drum interlude. There Must Be a Song Like You by Helado Negro
  • Proggy guitar+piano loop Striking Down Your Big Premiere by Ryley Walker
  • When the drums and guitar arpeggios drop. Up & Down by Crumb
  • Rapid layered strumming outro. Liberty Bell by Darkside
  • Sudden quiet synth outro. Sunrise/Sunset by The Dodos
  • Unexpected airhorn in an indie pop coda. I‘m Not Home by Another Michael
  • Piano denouement. I See You by Aimee Mann

Part 3: Vocal Delivery and Effects

  • “You went and got yourself off yourself… off.” Weird Carolina by Camp trash
  • Our loop. Loop by Sophia Kennedy
  • The “annnnnd…” held and vibrato’d. Grow by Shanique Marie
  • “I can see my life so cl—Burn out, burn out” Reality check. Musician by Porter Robinson
  • Multi-part harmony and chugging guitar. 29 by Yaeji & OHHYUK
  • “I guess I’ll have to fight you with a guitar.” With a Guitar by The Dodos
  • Lyrics spilling over the line and background harmony behind “I gotta change what is to come/My fears glowing in-between time…” I Can See You by Sophia Kennedy
  • Perfectly cut-off interview recording. Indigo Grit by Space Afrika
  • “Downatthemeninmusic BUSINESS conference!” desperately whisper-sung. White Dress by Lana Del Rey
  • “Science fiction apocalypse” and the accompanying background scream. Orange Tic Tac by Sophia Kennedy
  • “‘Excuse me.’     ‘…What.’” Chaise Longue by Wet Leg
  • “Wh—where—are—my—trumpet-trumpet ohhh” *makes trumpet noises.* Same Size Shoe by serpentwithfeet
  • Big autotuned backup vocal. Dead Right Now by Lil Nas X
  • High synth line, echoing and ducking the kick drum. Cat on My Tongue by Sophia Kennedy
  • “The blood… the stream… the blood… the stream…” rolling on and on. Aurora by Bachelor
  • Sudden, plaintive, lightly-phasered vocals. Flowers by Elkka
